The Great Wall of China

Unique Places to See:

History :

  1. The Great Wall of China is one of the Seven Wonders of the World, the most iconic attraction in China, and the leading wonder among the Seven Wonders of China. Stretching an astonishing 5,500 miles (8,850 km) across the country, it stands as one of the most remarkable feats of engineering in human history.

    The wall itself is comprised of 3,889 miles (6,259 km) of man-made fortifications. The remaining distance is accounted for by 223 miles (359 km) of trenches and 1,387 miles (2,232 km) of natural barriers, including mountains and rivers that were incorporated into the defense system.

    When considering all branches of the wall, its total length extends to an astonishing 13,171 miles (21,196 km), making it one of the greatest architectural achievements in history.

The Terracotta Army :

  1. The Terracotta Army stands alongside the Great Wall of China as one of the Seven Wonders of Asia and remains one of the most celebrated historical and cultural attractions in China and all of Asia.

    This vast collection of life-sized terracotta sculptures represents the armies of Qin Shi Huang, the first emperor of China, and was created as funerary art to accompany him in the afterlife.

    The Terracotta Army consists of over 8,000 soldiers, 130 chariots pulled by 520 horses, and an additional 150 cavalry horses. These figures were buried with the emperor between 210–209 BCE as part of a vast necropolis that spans 38 square miles (98 sq. km).

    Each terracotta figure is life-sized and uniquely detailed, varying in uniform, height, hairstyle, and facial features—of which at least 10 distinct face shapes have been identified.

Forbidden City :

  1. Located in the heart of Beijing, the Forbidden City is an architectural masterpiece that served as the imperial palace from 1420 to 1912, spanning the Ming and Qing dynasties.

    The vast palace complex consists of 980 buildings spread across 180 acres (72 ha), making it one of the largest and most well-preserved palace complexes in the world.

    Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Forbidden City is home to the largest collection of preserved ancient wooden structures. As one of China’s most popular attractions, it welcomes 16.7 million visitors annually.

    The Forbidden City is structured as a massive rectangle, measuring 3,153 feet (961 m) from north to south and 2,470 feet (753 m) from east to west. It houses an incredible 8,888 bays of rooms, all enclosed within 26-foot (7.9 m) high walls and surrounded by a 171-foot (52 m) wide moat.
